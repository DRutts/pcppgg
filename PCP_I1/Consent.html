Study for Decision Making in a Group

Principal Investigator: David Ruttenberg, PhD Student, University of Michigan School of Information

You are invited to participate in a research study about how people make decisions in a group setting.

If you agree to be part of the research study, you will participate in a simple economic game.

Benefits of the research include making decisions in a group setting and earning money based on the choices you make.

Risks and discomforts include uncertainty about final earnings.

Compensation consists of a show-up payment of $5 with the chance to earn additional payment, up to $6.90.

Participating in this study is completely voluntary. Even if you decide to participate now, you may change your mind and stop at any time. You may choose not to continue with the experiment at any time for any reason.

We will protect the confidentiality of your research records by deleting all unique identifying information after you receive the receipt for your payment.

Information collected in this project may be shared with other researchers, but we will not share any information that could identify you.
